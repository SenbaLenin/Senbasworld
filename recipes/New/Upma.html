Upma
(South Indian Rava Upma)

Ingredients

  • Rava (semolina) – 1 cup
  • Oil – 2½ tbsp
  • Mustard seeds – ½ tsp
  • Cumin seeds – ½ tsp
  • Urad dal – 1 tsp
  • Chana dal – 1½ tsp
  • Cashews – 10–12
  • Peanuts – 2 tbsp (optional)
  • Green chilies – 2, slit
  • Ginger – ½ inch, grated
  • Curry leaves – 1 sprig
  • Asafoetida (hing) – a pinch
  • Onion – ¼ cup, finely chopped
  • Mixed vegetables carrot, peas, potato– ½ cup (optional)
  • Water – 2 1/2 cups
  • Salt – ¾ tsp (adjust to taste)
  • Ghee – 2 tsp(optional)
  • Tomoto – 1 (Optional)
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Method

1. Roast Rava: Dry roast rava on medium heat until aromatic and slightly crunchy. Do not brown. Set aside.
2. Tempering: Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds, cumin, urad dal, chana dal, and cashews. Fry until golden.
3. Add Aromatics: Add hing, green chilies, ginger, curry leaves, and chopped onion. Sauté until onions turn translucent. Add Tomoto and vegetables if using and cook until slightly tender.
4. Boil Water: Add 3 cups water and salt. Bring to boil. Taste and adjust salt.
5. Add Rava: Lower heat. Slowly pour roasted rava in a steady stream while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
6. Steam: Cover and cook on low flame for 2–4 minutes until water is absorbed. Add ghee and stir gently.
7. Finish: Turn off heat. Rest covered for 5 minutes.
8. Serve: Top with fresh coriander and Serve hot with coconut chutney, pickle, or curd.

🔄 Variation Note

To make Vermicelli Upma (Semiya Upma), use vermicelli instead of rava (semolina).

  • Replace rava with 1 cup vermicelli.
  • Use 1 1/4 cups water for every cup of vermicelli.
  • Dry roast vermicelli until golden before cooking.

Method (Short Version)

  1. Dry roast vermicelli until golden; set aside.
  2. Heat oil, add mustard seeds, dals, cashews, and fry till golden.
  3. Add green chilies, ginger, curry leaves, onion; sauté until soft.
  4. Pour in 1½ cups water and salt; bring to boil.
  5. Add roasted vermicelli slowly, stir continuously.
  6. Cover and cook on low for 2–3 minutes until water is absorbed.
  7. Garnish with Cooriender; fluff and serve hot.

📝 **Note:** When making cracked wheat upma, use a ratio of **1 glass cracked wheat to 3 glasses water** for a soft, well-cooked texture. Adjust slightly if you prefer it firmer or more porridge-like.Upma is a timeless South Indian breakfast!
