Pulishadam
(Tamarind Rice)

Ingredients

  • Tamarind – lemon-sized ball
  • Oil – 2 tbsp (sesame preferred)
  • Mustard seeds – 1 tsp
  • Urad dal – 1 tsp
  • Channa dal – 1 tbsp
  • Peanuts – 2 tbsp
  • Curry leaves – few
  • Asafoetida – a pinch
  • Turmeric powder – 1/4 tsp
  • Salt – to taste
  • Jaggery – 1 tsp
  • Methi powder – 1/4 tsp
  • Cooked rice – as needed

Method

1. Tamarind extraction: Soak the lemon-sized tamarind in hot water for 10–15 minutes. Squeeze and extract thick tamarind juice, discarding pulp and seeds.
2. Seasoning: Heat oil. Add mustard seeds (let them splutter), urad dal, channa dal, and peanuts. Fry until golden. Add curry leaves and asafoetida, sauté lightly.
3. Tamarind Mixture: Add tamarind extract, salt, and turmeric. Boil the mixture until it thickens and reduces (about 8–10 min).
4. Finishing: Add methi powder and jaggery. Mix and boil for another 5 min.
5. Serve: Mix with cooked rice. Adjust salt. Drizzle with extra sesame oil if you like.
6. Storage: You can prepare the Pulishadam mix ahead of time and Store your in an airtight container in the fridge for about a month. For longer storag. At room temperature, it will last 2–3 days. Proper storage and clean handling are key to keeping it fresh and flavorful.

Enjoy your flavorful Pulishadam!
