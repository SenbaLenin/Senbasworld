🥥 Coimbatore-Style Parupu Sadham

A comforting and flavorful rice dish made with toor dal, coconut masala, and aromatic spices—straight from the heart of Kongu Nadu.

🌾 Ingredients

  • ½ glass cooked toor dal
  • 1 glass rice (short grain preferred)
  • ¼ cup grated coconut
  • 1 small piece cinnamon
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds (sombu)
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1 small piece ginger
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • A few curry leaves
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 green chili, slit
  • ½ tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp urad dal
  • 2 tsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water

👩‍🍳 Method

  1. Grind coconut, cinnamon, fennel seeds, curry leaves, ginger, garlic, and green chilies into a smooth paste.
  2. Heat oil in a cooker. Add mustard seeds, urad dal, curry leaves and fennel seeds. Let them splutter.
  3. Add onions and sauté until golden. Add tomatoes and green chili. Cook until soft.
  4. Mix in the ground masala paste and cooked toor dal. Stir well and cook for 2–3 minutes.
  5. Add washed rice and 2 cups of water(Total water ratio with grinded paste+dal water all together). Season with salt.
  6. Cover and simmer for 8 minutes or cook for 1 whistle in a pressure cooker.
  7. Let it rest. Fluff gently and serve hot.

🍽️ Serving Suggestions

  • Thayir Pachadi (Yogurt Raita) – Cucumber or onion-based raita adds a cooling contrast.
  • Appalam or Vadam – Crispy sides for texture.
  • Lemon Pickle – A tangy kick to balance the flavors.
  • Ghee – A drizzle of melted ghee elevates the aroma and taste.
