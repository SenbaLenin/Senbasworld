🥬 Palak Thambuli

Ingredients

  • 1 handful of palak (spinach) leaves – chopped
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 green chilli (slit) or 1/2 tsp pepper
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut
  • 1 cup buttermilk (1/2 cup curd + 1/2 cup water)
  • 1/2 dry red chilli
  • 2 tsp cooking oil or ghee
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • Salt as per taste

Preparation

  1. Heat a frying pan and add 1 tsp oil or ghee.
  2. Add cumin seeds and slit green chilli. When the cumin splutters, add chopped palak leaves.
  3. Fry until the leaves turn soft and the raw smell disappears.
  4. Turn off the heat and allow the mixture to cool completely.
  5. Take grated coconut in a mixie jar. Add the cooled palak mixture and salt.
  6. Grind into a smooth paste by adding required water.
  7. Transfer the paste into a container.
  8. Add buttermilk (whisked curd + water) and mix well.
  9. Add extra water if needed — the consistency should be thin like a light milkshake.

Seasoning (Tadka)

  1. Heat 1 tsp oil or ghee.
  2. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add 1/2 dry red chilli and fry briefly.
  4. Pour the seasoning over the thambuli and mix gently.

Serve chilled with hot rice. A cooling, healthy Karnataka-style thambuli perfect for summer.
