Poricha Kuzhambu

A comforting South Indian lentil and vegetable stew, rich in flavor and gentle on the soul.

Ingredients

  • Brinjal, carrot, lima beans, green beans, potato, zucchini, plantain – cut into small pieces
  • Salt – to taste
  • Turmeric powder – ¼ tsp
  • Sambar powder – 1 tsp
  • Moong dal – 1 cup (boiled)
  • Coconut – ¼ cup (grated)
  • Curry leaves – a few
  • Cumin seeds – 1 tsp
  • Vadagam – optional

Tempering Ingredients

  • Mustard seeds – 1 tsp
  • Urad dal – 1 tsp
  • Dried red chilies – 2 (broken)
  • Curry leaves – a sprig
  • Asafoetida (hing) – a pinch
  • Oil – 1 tbsp (coconut oil preferred)

Preparation Steps

  • Boil chopped vegetables with salt, turmeric, and sambar powder until tender.
  • Add boiled moong dal and simmer for 5 minutes.
  • Grind coconut, curry leaves, and cumin into a smooth paste. Stir it into the stew and cook for 5 more minutes.
  • Heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add urad dal and fry until golden, followed by red chilies and curry leaves.
  • Sprinkle asafoetida, stir quickly, and pour the tempering over the kuzhambu. Mix well.
  • If using vadagam, temper it separately and mix in at the end.

Serving suggestion: Perfect with hot steamed rice and crispy papad.
