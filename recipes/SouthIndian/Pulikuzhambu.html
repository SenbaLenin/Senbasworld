Pulikuzhambu

The magic of Pulikuzhambu lies in its irresistibly tangy depth—sour, spicy, and utterly sublime. A timeless South Indian classic, it’s best served with hot, fluffy steamed rice.

Ingredients

Tamarind Mix

  • Lemon-sized tamarind ball
  • 2–3 tsp sambar powder
  • Salt to taste
  • ¼ tsp turmeric powder

Seasoning & Base

  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp urad dal
  • 1 tsp toor dal
  • ½ tsp fenugreek seeds (methi)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds (jeera)
  • 15 to 20 garlic cloves
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • ¼ tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 3–4 small brinjals (eggplants), chopped
  • 1 tomato or 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp jaggery (optional)

Coconut Paste

  • ¼ cup grated coconut
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • A few curry leaves

Instructions

  1. Soak tamarind in warm water and extract the juice. Mix in sambar powder, salt, and turmeric powder.
  2. Bring the tamarind mixture to a boil and let it simmer for a few minutes to develop flavor.
  3. In parallel, heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds, urad dal, toor dal, fenugreek seeds, and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  4. Add lightly crushed garlic and chopped onion. Sauté until onions are soft. Stir in asafoetida and chopped brinjal. Cook briefly.
  5. Pour the simmered tamarind mix into the seasoned pan. Add tomato or tomato paste and let it boil until brinjal is tender.
  6. Grind coconut, cumin seeds, and curry leaves into a smooth paste.
  7. Stir in the coconut paste and jaggery. Simmer for 5–7 minutes to meld the flavors.
  8. Serve hot with rice, and enjoy with papad or pickle on the side.
